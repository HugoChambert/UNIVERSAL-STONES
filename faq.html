Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about our stonework services

Granite is composed primarily of quartz, feldspar, and mica and represents one of earth's hardest materials. It resists scratches, acids, stains, and heat while offering diverse colors and patterns. Your granite countertop is unique and adds lasting value to your home.

Marble's calcite composition creates beautiful veining and colors suitable for bathroom design. However, it's vulnerable to acidic substances like vinegar, lemon, tomatoes, and wine, so it requires careful maintenance to preserve its beauty.

Each quarried block and resulting slab differs naturally. Your countertop is unique because of these inherent variations in natural stone. These natural variations are what make each piece special and one-of-a-kind.

Start with color selection, then choose edge profiles. Rounded or beveled edges resist chipping better than squared edges, though complexity increases cost. Our team can guide you through the selection process to find the perfect match for your space.

Natural stone surpasses synthetics in value and character. No man-made surface can match the depth and character of natural stone and maintains home value. Natural stone provides a timeless beauty that synthetic materials simply cannot replicate.

Both clean easily with warm water, mild soap, and soft cloths. Granite is worry-free; marble requires extra caution around citric acids, alcohols, and oils. We provide detailed care instructions after installation to help you maintain your countertops properly.

Different stain types require specific treatments: oil stains need detergent or acetone; organic stains need hydrogen peroxide; metal stains require poultice; ink and paint have targeted solutions. Contact us for specific guidance based on your stain type.

Light scratches respond to steel wool. Deeper damage requires professional repair and repolishing. Our team can assess the damage and provide the appropriate solution to restore your countertop's appearance.

Water carrying mineral salts from below the surface of the stone evaporates, leaving powder residue. Use a vacuum or dust mop repeatedly as drying occurs. This is a normal occurrence and will diminish over time.
